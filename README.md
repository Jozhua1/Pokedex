⚡ Trainer’s Pokedex

An interactive Pokedex app for Pokémon trainers, built with React and TypeScript. Trainers can discover Pokémon from all generations, analyze abilities, build teams, compare Pokémon stats, and communicate with other trainers inside the app.

✨ Technologies
React
TypeScript
Tailwind CSS
Vite
Framer Motion (for smooth UI animations)
🚀 Features
Discover Pokémon: Search and explore Pokémon across all generations.
Favorite Pokémon: Save your top Pokémon to a personal favorites list.
Team Builder: Strategically create your Pokémon team and analyze stats.
Compare Pokémon: Compare stats, types, and abilities side-by-side.
Abilities Analyzer: Dive into Pokémon abilities and understand their effects.
Trainer Messaging: Chat and connect with other Pokémon trainers in-app.
Responsive Design: Works seamlessly on desktops and mobile devices.
📍 The Process

I wanted to build a Pokedex that was more than just a reference tool — a hub for trainers. Starting with React and TypeScript, I structured the app around easy-to-use components for searching, comparing, and favoriting Pokémon.

The team builder and abilities analyzer give trainers a way to plan strategies and understand their Pokémon better. Messaging functionality lets trainers interact and share tips inside the app. Framer Motion adds subtle animations for smooth interactions, making the app feel lively and engaging.

It was important to make everything responsive, so trainers on both desktop and mobile can enjoy exploring Pokémon without compromise.
